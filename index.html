Currently, I am a Research Fellow at the Athinoula A. Martinos Center for Biomedical Imaging, working under superversion of Dr. Iman Aganj and co-mentored by Dr. Bruce Fischl. My research focuses on developing advanced harmonization methods for brain connectivity, specifically addressing critical challenges in multi-site neuroimaging studies.

Before joining the Martinos Center, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Center for Biomedical Image Computing and Analytics at the University of Pennsylvania, where I led comprehensive research focused on understanding brain aging through multiscale functional connectivity analysis. I also had the chance to work with Dr. Dinggang Shen and Dr. Han Zhang at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill during my last two years of Ph.D., where I developed BrainNetClass toolbox and helped develop the first infant-dedicated resting-state fMRI data analysis pipeline in the Baby Connectome Project.
